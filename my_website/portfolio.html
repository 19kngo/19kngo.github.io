Reflection

Purpose: The purpose of this game is to collect as many points as possible. For this game, I focused particularly on the movements of my sprites to ensure it ran smoothly. Two crucial elements of my game were the independent movement of the ghosts as well as the movement of the Pac Man sprite itself. Each ghost sprite had to move independently of one another and Pac-Man and to navigate through the maze, something I could not just randomly set the position of the sprite to. I instead selected certain coordinates for the sprite to glide to in a set duration of time. To increase efficiency and decrease my work load, I set each ghost sprite to have the same movements though at different starting points corresponding to the player's current score. I also had to establish the Pac-Man's movement so that it resembled the original game in which the Pac-Man character opened and closed his mouth repeatedly and moved continuously through the maze. To incorporate these aspects, I created costumes of the sprite to switch between an open mouthed and close mouthed character. For the continous movement, I simply set the Pac-Man movement to forever.

I encountered several difficulties through the development of both games. With the first game, I had limited knowledge of how to use Scratch as well as all of its functions and scripts. In the second game, my still-limited knowledge required me to spend extensive time on each aspect and limited what I was able to complete and what my sprites were able to do. One specific example was Pac-Man's movement through the walls of the maze. My original intention was for Pac Man to navigate the maze by itself by setting its movement only when it was touching black rather than the blue maze walls. However, this did not work, allowing Pac-Man to move through the walls of the maze. I simply chose to incorporate this aspect into the game as I was unable to figure out how to change it until I develop a more advanced understanding of Scratch.
